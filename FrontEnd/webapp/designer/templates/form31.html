3.1 Select Content

About this Phase

At first, the insights and gaps revealed in Phase 2 have to be translated by your CDP team in order to allow for a discussion on new contents that might become relevant to your curriculum. While the activities in Phase 2 indicate potential gaps, e.g. a lack of certain technologies that are requested by practice but do not show up in your curriculum.

However, these discussion on potentially relevant content shall always be backed by your rationale and the eventual goal of your institution. The discussion within your teams has to consider questions such as:

  • What content can fill the gap(s) identified by the curriculum analysis?
  • Does this content prove to comply with the rationale of the curriculum / the institution?
  • Is the new content a temporary development that might be irrelevant in the near future?

At the end of your discussion you should agree on a list of relevant content that might enhance your given curriculum or constitute the new curriculum. In alignment with these contents a clear link to the learning objectives of your curriculum has to be established. Which objectives are achieved through the inclusion of the specific content?

Note: For the given prototype, the tool is assessing job ads from the field of Data Science. The applicability is so for only given for related programmes. Yet, the applicability can be expanded by means of integrating different sources from different fields of job types.
