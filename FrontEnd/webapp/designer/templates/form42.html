4.2 Evaluate Curriculum

About this Phase

Based on the reactions from the stakeholders involved or affected by the new curriculum design, you should set up a structured evaluation building on top of this initial comments, that might emerge from the prior step.

In order to fully evaluate the design you might consider multiple options. Of course, the most realistic and valuable means of evaluation is the actual run of a pilot. Deploying the full curriculum as a test run, though, might be infeasible based on examination regulations, lack of skilled researches and lacking long-term preparation for holding new courses. Of course these options are influenced by the degree of change that has been imposed on the curriculum. In case of only minor changes, you might have no problems in running a new module, or even only a single lecture within a module of your new curriculum.

A second means of evaluating the curriculum can be found in expert and stakeholder discussions. This can be set up as larger workshops including discussions and open questions addressing the new curriculum. The stakeholders involved should be invited to such rounds, just as representatives of stakeholders that are affected by the curriculum.

References

  • Zhao, D.; Ma, X.; Qiao, S. (2016): What aspects should be evaluated when evaluating graduate curriculum: Analysis based on student interview. Studies in Educational Evaluation 54.
  • Matkovic, Predrag; Tumbas, Pere; Sakal, Marton; Pavlićević, Veselin (2014): Stakeholders in the Analysis Phase of Curriculum Development Process Model.
