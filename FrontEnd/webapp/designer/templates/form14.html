1.4 Set up Communication Plan

About this phase

Having derived your objectives for the new curriculum, it is important to set up a suitable communication plan. The design of a curriculum is a complex process, involving a plethora of different parties. As identified before in step 1.2, all relevant stakeholders need to be addressed according to their needs for information and their demand for participation in the decision process.

Without a structured overview of whom to talk to, involve or inform, the design of a new curriculum will result in a chaotic and confusing flow of information, predominantly being requested rather than proactively communicated. Depending on the specific stakeholder, such a delayed dissemination of information can lead to negative consequences for your overall project.

Drawing from the field of management and communication, you will be provided with a template to create your own communication plan to avoid the above described scenario. This document helps you to keep track of all the parties, i.e. stakeholders, involved in the design process and assigning the right communication patterns according to the stakeholders’ needs. Below you will find the template that specifies for each of the stakeholders, when to contact them with what information by means of which channel. Before proceeding please fill out the attached stakeholder map (See: Resources).

The benefits of sticking to your communication plan will help to avoid confusion and disharmonies among the many participants involved in the design process. Please note that a carefully communicated intention to change or introduce a new curriculum will potentially face high levels of acceptance as long as decisions are made transparent and relevant parties are involved and informed from the very inception of the process.

A side note: Both the communication plan, as well as the prior created stakeholder map leave room for your specific needs. As mere templates, these documents cannot prescribe specific stakeholders or even people, since this tool is ought to support a broad variety of institutional context, which may differ tremendously.

Furthermore, it is highly advised to continuously update the documents. Both stakeholders’ interest and power may change within the dynamic context of your institution. Continuously checking your communication plans during the whole project and assess their validity is a repetitive but important task to be performed by the internal team.
