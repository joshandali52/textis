3.2 Integrate Content

About this Phase

Eventually, step 3.1. should result in a list of new content that shall be included in your adapted curriculum. Most importantly this list shall be agreed upon by all the relevant stakeholders (cf. step 1.4.).

In this next step, it is important to integrate the identified content into your existing curriculum. This includes creating a time-logical order of teaching and embedding the content within the right and effective means of teaching.

This process is highly specific to the actual content selected in the prior step and cannot be generalized without making certain constraints. Yet, the following questions shall guide the process of integrating new content into your existing curriculum.

  • Which anchors exist in the given curriculum to which the new content can be linked?
  • At what moment of time shall the the new content be included?
  • Which content is required to be covered a priori in order to ease the learning experience?
  • What type of teaching is best suited to convey the new content?
  • Which passages of the old curriculum can be substituted by the new content or have to be alerted to align to the new content?

References

  • Short, E. D. (1986): A Historical Look at Curriculum Design. Theory Into Practice 25 (1).
  • Boschman, F.; McKenney, S.; Voogt, J. (2014): Understanding decision making in teachers’ curriculum design approaches. Educational Technology Research and Development 62.
