1.3 Derive Objectives

About this Phase

Based on the overall rationale behind your future, new or updated curriculum, the logical next step is to derive more concrete objectives of your design process. In order to do so, it is important to align the objectives with the prior identified needs. Having formulated a rationale behind the overall curriculum design process, needs and shortcomings of the existing (or not yet existing) curriculum have to be considered. Mirroring these needs with specific goals helps you in setting clear cut objectives.

Settled process models for the design of curricula have come forth with guidelines on how to derive specific objectives. In fact, scholars regard the definition of objectives – next to the initial definition of your rationale – as the most important step of the overall CDP (Tyler, ADDIE). They share the approach to start by identifying the needs of the core audience of the new curriculum. This task is supported by the prior defined stakeholder map. To design a comprehensive and suitable curriculum, you should consider all stakeholders and address their needs. At foremost, self-evidently, the students’ needs should be of strongest concern to any new curriculum design process. The internal design team should consider the students’ abilities and their wishes, respectively critique, which can be sourced from prior end-of-year course evaluations. Imagining what students will eventually achieve and do in their lives as they pass the new curriculum. This can be a supporting thought in specifying the objectives (Taba).

Before continuing: Set up and discuss a list of concrete objectives that support your rationale and respect the needs of the CDP’s stakeholders, especially considering the future students.
