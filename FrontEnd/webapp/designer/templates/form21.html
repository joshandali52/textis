2.1 Assess Status Quo

About this phase

The first activity of the second phase gives you the opportunity to build upon your rationale and conduct an in-depth assessment of why you want to or should change your curriculum. While deriving your rationale-included thoughts on why you want to change your curriculum at a general level, this assessment should be more detailed.

Let us start with a common example most lecturers will know about: Students have provided feedback complaining about a lack of topical, and new contents that they might have become aware of outside the lecture hall. This lack of new and fresh content resulted in a poor evaluation, so you became willing to change and adapt your curriculum under the rationale of providing topical, state of the art material within your curriculum.

Following this example, it is now your task to check the given state of your curriculum. Motivated by your rationale, it is advisable to start assessing your curriculum by checking the age of your materials and resources that inform your courses. The questions to ask at this stage are highly dependent on your rationale, yet some generally applicable questions to answer in this activity are:

  • Which modules have not been adapted and updated in a long period (e.g. 2-3 years)?
  • Which modules, or even individual lectures, have received poor evaluation results from the students?
  • Which modules or lectures show the greatest potential for updates?

This time-perspective taken on in this example, is only one dimension in which your curriculum can be assessed. It is generally advisable to take on multiple perspectives, which go beyond your main reason for change. E.g. although students complained about topicality of your courses, it is worthwhile to consider the practical relevance of your curriculum.

In order to answer these questions and provide more information about the given status quo, a tool is provided which allows for three means of analysis (please click the links to use the tool; they will open in a new window):

  1. Syllables Analysis
  2. Word Compare
  3. Word Tree

All of the above-mentioned means of analysis build upon text mining algorithms that have analyzed a pool of hundreds of job ads, in order to allow for a deeper understanding of the actual practical relevance of the existing passages of your curriculum.

To allow for a high flexibility, these means of analysis allow you to insert any word or passage from your existing curriculum and provide you with detailed information, specific to the three types of analysis. Further guidelines on how to use the different tools most effectively can be found in the description of the analysis tools.

Note: For the given prototype, the tool is assessing job ads from the field of Data Science. The applicability is so for only given for related programmes. Yet, the applicability can be expanded by means of integrating different sources from different fields of job types.
