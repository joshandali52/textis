1.1 Declare Rationale

About this Phase

Considering your CDP, you decided to enhance and update an existing curriculum. Within such a process, any of the subsequent decisions that have to be made should be backed by a rationale, motivating and explaining why you want to update the existing curriculum, or create a whole new program. This rationale should state a clear goal of the planned program and be in line with the overarching vision and strategy of the broader institutional vision or strategy.

Some questions that help creating such a rationale for the new curriculum can be seen in the following examples, that tackle the motivation on why you might want to change an existing curriculum.

  • Keeping up with the pace of the changing environment
  • Unsatisfied students and poor course evaluation results
  • Request for change issued by the Chair / Faculty
  • Lacking practical relevance
  • Lacking theoretical foundation
  • Lacking topicality and keeping up with the research field’s advancements

Next to the reasons for change it should be considered in what ways the institutional context demands alignment with requirements stated by accreditation associations and standards, e.g. such as AACSB.
